What is Considered ICT?

Once you have determined your business need (the functionality you require), you must begin by determining if that need is considered ICT. ICT is defined by the U.S. Access Board.

Information technology and other equipment, systems, technologies, or processes, for which the principal function is the creation, manipulation, storage, display, receipt, or transmission of electronic data and information, as well as any associated content. Examples of ICT include, but are not limited to:

  • Computers and peripheral equipment
  • Information kiosks and transaction machines
  • Telecommunications equipment
  • Customer premises equipment
  • Multifunction office machines
  • Applications
  • Websites
  • Videos
  • Electronic documents

Mary's multifunction machine is definitely ICT.
