Approvers and reviewers play a critical role in preventing inaccessible content from being published.

<ol class="usa-process-list itad-process-list--wide">
  <li class="usa-process-list__item">
    <h2 class="usa-process-list__heading">Review Before Publishing</h2>
    <p class="margin-top-05">Accessibility review should occur before publication, not after complaints or remediation requests. Before approving content:</p>
    <ul class="usa-list">
      <li>Check the heading structure.</li>
      <li>Verify link text.</li>
      <li>Review alternative text.</li>
      <li>Test keyboard navigation.</li>
      <li>Confirm linked or uploaded document accessibility.</li>
      <li>Verify captions, audio descriptions, or transcripts.</li>
      <li>Review tables for proper structure.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>

  <li class="usa-process-list__item">
    <h2 class="usa-process-list__heading">Use Automated and Manual Testing Together</h2>
    <p class="margin-top-05">Automated testing tools, such as browser accessibility inspectors and CMS accessibility plugins, can help identify common accessibility issues but cannot detect all accessibility problems.</p>
    <p>Manual testing remains necessary for:</p>
    <ul class="usa-list">
      <li>Keyboard accessibility.</li>
      <li>Meaningful alternative text.</li>
      <li>Logical reading order.</li>
      <li>Link clarity.</li>
      <li>Focus management.</li>
    </ul>
    <p>Automated testing should support, not replace, manual review.</p>
    <p>Automated testing, manual testing, and technical conformance testing can identify many accessibility issues. For critical services, high-impact workflows, or frequently used public-facing content, agencies may also benefit from usability testing with people with disabilities to identify barriers that may not be detected through conformance testing alone.</p>
  </li>

  <li class="usa-process-list__item">
    <h2 class="usa-process-list__heading">Establish Governance and Standards</h2>
    <p class="margin-top-05">Strong governance improves long-term accessibility outcomes. Governance helps maintain consistent accessibility across distributed publishing teams and reduces inconsistent accessibility outcomes across teams.</p>
    <p>Agencies should establish:</p>
    <ul class="usa-list">
      <li>Approved component inventories.</li>
      <li>Accessible design patterns.</li>
      <li>Accessibility review checkpoints for content creation.</li>
      <li>Accessibility review procedures for new components and integrations.</li>
      <li>Accessibility review requirements for platform upgrades and major releases.</li>
      <li>Regression testing procedures following significant platform changes.</li>
      <li>Content publishing standards.</li>
      <li>Accessibility criteria for approving reusable templates and workflows.</li>
      <li>Author training requirements.</li>
      <li>Accessibility escalation procedures.</li>
      <li>Quality assurance surveillance plan procedures.</li>
      <li>Periodic audits of high-traffic content.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
</ol>
