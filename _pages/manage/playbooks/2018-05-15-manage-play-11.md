How to test and validate Section 508 and ICT accessibility conformance is described in Play 10. Play 11 addresses what to do with the list of issues or defects found during testing. Agencies should systematically track, prioritize, and resolve defects within specified timelines. End users may also report ICT accessibility issues as part of public feedback or Section 508-related complaints. Agencies should also assess, track, prioritize, and resolve reported issues. Addressing the root cause of an issue may incur costs for the agency, regardless of its origin. If the defect stems from a lack of Section 508 conformance, the agency should take measures to prevent its recurrence in future development, upgrades, and procurements.

## Checklists
This section provides checklists to guide systematic Section 508 conformance testing, planning, and process implementation for ICT throughout its lifecycle.

          <td>Develop a process to track and monitor Section 508 conformance:
            <ul>
              <li>For internal projects and software development, assess internal bug tracking systems for suitability in tracking accessibility defects.</li>
              <li>Consider adding Section 508 data to current <a href="{{ site.baseurl }}/tools/glossary/#g">governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) tools</a> used within your agency.</li>
              <li>Assess your current help desk suitability for tracking and resolving accessibility issues reported by end-users.</li>

          <td>Develop a process to assess and troubleshoot the issue:
            <ul>
              <li>Is the problem caused by the technology, third party components used by the product, the operating environment the product is deployed in, the assistive technology used to access the product, or some other source?</li>
              <li>Is the problem caused by a lack of training or communications?</li>
              <li>Is the problem caused by a usability issue?</li>
              <li>Is the problem caused by non-conformance with the <a href="https://www.access-board.gov/ict/" target="_blank" class="usa-link--external">Section 508 Standards</a>?</li>
            </ul>
          </td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <th scope="row"><svg class="usa-icon text-blue center-middle" aria-hidden="true" focusable="false" role="img"><use href="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/sprite.svg#check_box_outline_blank"></use></svg></th>
          <td>Develop a process to categorize accessibility issues. For example:
            <ul>
              <li>Issues cited in Section 508-related complaints: These items require formal, careful, expedited handling by the Section 508 Program, the IT department, general counsel, and possibly external Federal entities such as the Department of Justice (DOJ).</li>
              <li>Technical conformance issues identified through testing or informal user reports: Agencies should address these items through risk mitigation processes as discussed in <a href="{{site.baseurl}}/manage/playbooks/technology-accessibility-playbook/10/">Play 10</a> and can include <a href="{{ site.baseurl }}/test/elements-of-an-accessibility-test-report/">documented defects</a>.</li>
              <li>Deployment issues: These may result from challenges in how, where, and when agencies deploy ICT services and technologies.</li>
              <li>Public feedback: Public submissions, whether or not directly related to Section 508 non-conformance, should be evaluated by knowledgeable employees who can determine how to direct the feedback. Agencies should document, address and communicate resolution of these issues to the submitters.</li>
            </ul>
          </td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <th scope="row"><svg class="usa-icon text-blue center-middle" aria-hidden="true" focusable="false" role="img"><use href="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/sprite.svg#check_box_outline_blank"></use></svg></th>
          <td>Develop a process to prioritize defects based on user impact or criticality. For example:
            <ul>
              <li>Critical issues may prevent an end user from accessing or using the technology.</li>
              <li>Major issues may make it extremely difficult, but not impossible, for an end user to access or use the technology.</li>
              <li>Minor issues do not materially affect the use of the product, but may represent a technical defect.</li>
            </ul>          
          </td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <th scope="row"><svg class="usa-icon text-blue center-middle" aria-hidden="true" focusable="false" role="img"><use href="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/sprite.svg#check_box_outline_blank"></use></svg></th>
          <td>Develop an escalation process to set timelines and address the need for corrective action or remediation based on risk and impact on end users. Follow your agency’s acquisition processes for <a href="{{site.baseurl}}/tools/glossary/#cure-notice">cure notice</a>, show cause, or delinquency notices, where applicable.</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <th scope="row"><svg class="usa-icon text-blue center-middle" aria-hidden="true" focusable="false" role="img"><use href="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/sprite.svg#check_box_outline_blank"></use></svg></th>

        </tr>
        <tr>
          <th scope="row"><svg class="usa-icon text-blue center-middle" aria-hidden="true" focusable="false" role="img"><use href="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/sprite.svg#check_box_outline_blank"></use></svg></th>
          <td>Develop and implement a process for managing Section 508-related complaints.
            <ul>
              <li>Utilize <a href="{{site.baseurl}}/manage/laws-and-policies/section-508-complaints-best-practices/">best practices to create a repeatable Section 508 complaints process</a> which can be based on your agency&#39;s Section 504 Complaint Policy.</li>
              <li>Ensure simple means are in place for the public to <a href="{{ site.baseurl }}/manage/laws-and-policies/implementing-public-feedback-mechanism/">submit feedback on accessibility without filing a formal complaint</a>.</li>
              <li>Review the agency&#39;s Section 504 policies.
                <div class="grid-col-12 border-base radius-lg padding-1" style="border: 1px solid black; background-color: #f5f9fc;">
                  <strong>Note:</strong> <a href="{{site.baseurl}}/tools/glossary/#ra">Reasonable accommodations</a> may be appropriate even when ICT meets Section 508 Standards.
                </div>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <th scope="row"><svg class="usa-icon text-blue center-middle" aria-hidden="true" focusable="false" role="img"><use href="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/sprite.svg#check_box_outline_blank"></use></svg></th>
          <td>Ensure that established Section 508-related complaint and feedback mechanisms, along with other required resources, are published on the agency's <a href="{{site.baseurl}}/manage/laws-and-policies/website-accessibility-statement/">website accessibility statement</a>, as required by OMB Memorandum.
