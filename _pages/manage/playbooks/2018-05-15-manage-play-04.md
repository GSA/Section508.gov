The cornerstone of an accessibility program is an agency-wide Section 508 policy. The policy must identify how the agency will manage accessibility compliance through technology acquisition, development, authoring, configuration, deployment, and maintenance activities. The policy must define clear roles and responsibilities. The policy must also clarify the authorities and shared responsibilities with other agency stakeholders for implementing Section 508 across the agency.

          <td>An effective Section 508 policy will:
            <ul>
              <li>State the policy's purpose and the agency's commitments to ensuring equal access to information for people with disabilities.</li>
              <li>Identify associated laws and regulations.</li>
              <li>Identify how the Section 508 policy relates to other policies at the agency and department level, including technology, acquisition, and other accessibility policies related to the Rehabilitation Act.</li>
              <li>Define authorities, roles, and responsibilities for all program stakeholders and participants.</li>
              <li>Define how Section 508 exceptions are adjudicated and tracked through resolution.</li>
              <li>Define minimum expectations for how Section 508 compliance will be addressed in acquisition, development, authoring, configuration, deployment and maintenance activities.</li>
              <li>Define minimum expectations for when and how Section 508 conformance testing will be performed in accordance with the policies, processes, and lifecycle activities assessed in <a href="{{site.baseurl}}/manage/playbooks/technology-accessibility-playbook/2/">Play 2</a>.</li>
              <li>Define where stakeholders should go for Section 508 technical assistance.</li>
              <li><a href="{{site.baseurl}}/manage/laws-and-policies/section-508-complaints-best-practices/">Define how issues and complaints will be reported, assessed, tracked, and resolved</a></li>
              <li>Define how governance will be used to ensure the policy is being followed (for example, independent reviews, non-compliance approvals, stage gates, and process audits).</li>
              <li>Define how conformance with policy will be assessed, tracked, and monitored.</li>
              <li>Define terminology to reduce misunderstanding.</li>
            </ul>
          </td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <th scope="row" aria-label="5"><svg class="usa-icon text-blue center-middle" aria-hidden="true" focusable="false" role="img"><use href="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/sprite.svg#check_box_outline_blank"></use></svg></th>
          <td>A Section 508 policy should make it clear that Section 508 conformance and accessibility is a responsibility for all employees. For example:
            <ul>
              <li>Program managers must ensure that Requests for Proposals (RFPs) and Statements of Work (SOWs) call for Section 508 conformance in all digital service and technology deliverables; additionally consider inclusion of direct and explicit penalties in these SOWs and RFPs if Section 508 standards are not met by contract deliverables (such as rejection of the deliverable, potential termination of contract for repeated infractions, etc.).</li>
              <li>Procurement officials must ensure that all digital service and technology procurements are evaluated for Section 508 conformance.</li>
              <li>IT Project Managers must ensure all digital services and technology solutions are designed and tested for Section 508 conformance.</li>
              <li>Developers must design and develop digital services with accessible user interfaces.</li>
            </ul>
          </td>
        </tr>
