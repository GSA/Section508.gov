As products are developed for the benefit and use of US citizens, federal employees, and other groups that include people with disabilities, agencies should conduct user research with the people that will interact with the product or solution being developed or implemented. We must begin technology projects by identifying the needs of people with disabilities who use and support the technology, and account for the ways the technology will fit into their lives. We must understand their preferences and expectations for using government technology to ensure it works for all users, not just some users. Creating user personas, testing with people with disabilities, and incorporating [user stories]({{site.baseurl}}/develop/user-stories/) can be used to integrate user needs into the design process. Adopting [universal design]({{site.baseurl}}/develop/universal-design/) as a core development principle is another useful method for integrating accessibility needs. Universal design is a concept in which products and environments are designed to be usable by all people, to the greatest extent possible, without the need for adaptation or specialized design. These perspectives should not only inform technical and design decisions, but should be considered throughout the IT investment, acquisition, development, and deployment phases.
