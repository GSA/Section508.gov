## About IAAF
The annual Interagency Accessibility Forum (IAAF) provides a venue for federal accessibility professionals, buyers, developers, designers, project managers, leadership and many others to exchange best practices in managing and implementing digital accessibility standards and reporting required by Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, as amended (29 USC 794d).

## 2025 IAAF
The 2025 Interagency Accessibility Forum (IAAF) will take place virtually on Zoom for Government from May 20-22. The event is hosted by the Government-wide IT Accessibility Program within the Office of Government-wide Policy (OGP) at the General Services Administration (GSA). Co-sponsors include the Federal CIO Council’s Accessibility Community of Practice (ACOP) and the U.S. Access Board (USAB), in collaboration with other federal agencies.

## Call for Presentations
The IAAF Planning Committee invites federal government employees to submit proposals to lead a workshop, presentation, or panel discussion.

### Submission Instructions
For all submissions, please prepare description of your session, and indicate your preferred format and thematic area(s). The submission deadline is **Friday, March 14th**. Please email your proposal to [IAAF@gsa.gov](mailto:IAAF@gsa.gov?subject=2025%20IAAF%20Presentation%20Proposal) and include:

* Employer
* Job Title
* Biography (100-150 words)
* Your general presentation experience and whether you’ve given this presentation before
* Session description (250 - 750 words)
* Learning objectives
* Target audience
* Desired session length (45, 60, 90, or 120 minutes)
* Co-presenter information, if applicable

All submissions will be reviewed by the Government-wide IT Accessibility Program and its partners based on the number of proposals received, available presentation slots, and how well the topic aligns with the conference theme and objectives. Submitting a proposal does not guarantee a speaking opportunity. 

Please note that speaking opportunities are voluntary and considered an in-kind contribution. You will not be paid or reimbursed for any expense.

### Potential Session Topics
This year's event will focus on the theme: Accessible by Design: Enhancing Efficiency in Federal ICT. Presentations should help participants understand and integrate Section 508 standards and best practices into their agency information and communication technologies. By sharing knowledge and practical strategies, this event aims to empower agencies to create more accessible and compliant digital environments.

Want to present but need an idea? Check out the topics below, or feel free to submit a proposal on a different subject. Your submission can cover multiple topics.

#### Acquisition & Compliance
* Writing clear solicitation language for accessibility
* Incorporating Section 508 into FITARA requirements
* Developing Quality Assurance Surveillance Plans (QASPs)

#### Design, Development & Testing
* Defining accessibility requirements in development
* Automated vs. manual accessibility testing
* CI/CD testing for Section 508 compliance
* Usability testing with assistive technology
* Testing mobile applications for accessibility
* Ensuring accessibility in GIS and interactive maps
* Certification and Accreditation (C&A) for accessibility
* Strategies for Enhancing Quality and Minimizing Remediation Efforts

#### Content Creation & Digital Media
* Applying Section 508 standards to Generative AI
* Creating accessible electronic documents
* Ensuring accessibility in videos and multimedia
* How to create captions and transcripts
* Writing effective alternative text for images
* Accessibility considerations for social media
* Designing accessible Learning Management Systems (LMS) and online training

#### Accessibility Management & Policy
* Integrating Section 508 into policy reviews
* Navigating the exceptions process
* Handling accessibility complaints
* Best practices for remediation and compliance tracking

### Submission Details
Authors of successful submissions will be notified by **March 28, 2025**, with final presentations due by **April 25, 2025**. Authors will be provided with a PowerPoint template for creating a consistent, accessible format for all conference presentations.

The presentations for this event will be published online and can be made available in advance of the event upon request.
Most plenary and panel sessions will be 60 minutes, but longer sessions can be arranged if needed. Since this event is virtual, we encourage presenters to include interactive elements in their sessions.

The available presentation formats are:

* Presentations
* Panels
* Workshops — intended to be an interactive session, where participants actively engage in exercises, activities, and demonstrations. Likely to be longer than 60 minutes

### Notes for those submitting presentation proposals
The event organizers will schedule an instructional webinar (date TBD) to help authors with developing and delivering accessible presentations.
Authors will receive acceptance notifications on March 28. Those with an accepted session or workshop must submit their final presentation by April 25.

This event will be recorded and may be posted on Section508.gov.
